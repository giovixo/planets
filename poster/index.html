Stability of planetary motion in binary star systems

De Cesare, Giovanni (INAF); Capuzzo-Dolcetta, Roberto (University of Rome); Marino, Alessio (University of Rome)

Abstract
Many exoplanets have been discovered in binary star systems, on internal (S-type) or circumbinary (P-type) orbits. In this study, we considered for the first time the problem of stability for planets of finite (non negligible) mass. Previous studies did not use a high precision integration scheme, and assumed the mass of the planet as negligible. We approached the problem by selecting a huge set of initial conditions for planetary orbits of the S-type, to perform high precision, very extended in time integrations at varying: the planet mass, its distance from the primary star, the mass ratio for the two stars in the system, and the eccentricity of the initial star motion. For our numerical integrations, we resorted to the use of a 15th order integration scheme (IAS15, available within the REBOUND framework), that provides an optimal solution for long-term time integrations. We estimated the probability of different types of instability: planet collisions with the primary or secondary star or planet ejected away from the binary star system. We confirm and generalize to massive planets the dependence of the critical semi-major axis on eccentricity and mass ratio of the binary already found by Holman and Wiegert (1999). We were also able to pick a significant number of orbits that are only marginally stable, according to the classification introduced by Museliak et al. (2005). Another, natural, extension of our previous work has been the study of perturbations induced to circumbinary planet motion by a passing by star, like it often happens in a star cluster. We performed a series of more than 4500 scattering experiments with different initial conditions typical of encounters in small star clusters. We found some interesting behaviors of the systems after perturbation and showed how a transition from an inner (S-type) stable orbit to a circumbinary (P-type) (and vice-versa) has a low probability.

Introduction

Figure 1: An artistic view of the planetary orbits in a binary star system. Credit: astronomy.com (Roen Kelly)

Orbits of planets in binary systems (Fig. 1) are traditionally classified into three categories (Dvorak 1986): i) the Planet-type (P-type) external orbits around both stars in the binary, ii) the Satellite type (S-type) internal orbits, around one of the two stars, and iii) the Libration-type (L-type) orbits (T-type in Fig. 1), corresponding to librations around the Lagrangian equilibrium points.

In this poster, we report some outcomes of the numerical study of:

  • the stability of S-type orbits of planets with a negligible mass as well as with a mass of the order of one or 30 Jupiter mass;
  • the consequences of a star scattering with the planetary system, with different initial conditions typical of encounters in small star clusters.
Two publications with more details and results on these topics are in preparation.

Model

Figure 2: An example stable planetary orbity (cyan) shown in the frame of the primary star (red circle) . The secondary star, at the distance of 10 AU from the primary star, is not shown. The blue circle is the unperturbed orbit, i.e. the orbit of the planet if there was no secondary star.

An example of stable planetary orbit is shown in Fig. 2. As a demostration, we also created a prototype of a real time orbit simulator. To cover a large set of different configurations, we made long term numerical integrations of a large number (more than ten thousand) of planetary orbits, using the high precision 15th order integration scheme IAS15, available whitin the REBOUND code. The stability of the orbits depends mainly on the initial distance of the planet from the primary star, on the mass ratio of the two star, and on the eccentricity of the binary star system. In this study, we assume that the motion of the planet is co-planar with the stars motion; we also set to zero the initial eccentricity of the planet motion.
